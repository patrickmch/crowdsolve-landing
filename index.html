What you’ll walk away with.

Clarity on what you’re actually building

You’ve got the idea. Maybe even a rough pitch. But is it sharp enough to put in front of real people? The curriculum helps you nail down your problem statement, solution hypothesis, and who your customer actually is — so you stop building on assumptions.

Focus when everything feels urgent

A thousand shiny objects competing for your attention. The program gives you a set of guidelines that cuts through the noise and moves your business forward — not just your to-do list.

People who actually get it

Not a Slack group. Not a LinkedIn connection. A small cohort of founders who are building right alongside you — bouncing ideas, sharing wins, and holding each other accountable.

Outside perspective that changes your direction

When you’re heads down, you miss things. The feedback and coaching help you see the core components you’ve been glossing over — before they become expensive mistakes.

A launchpad to funding

CrowdSolve connects you to mentors, investors, and the networks that matter at your stage. Climate founders who complete the program are also eligible for non-dilutive grant funding through our partner 1000 Gretas — no equity taken.

10 weeks. One clear path.

Weeks 1–3

Sharpen Your Idea

Nail your problem statement, solution hypothesis, and founder fit. Know what you’re building and why you’re the one to build it.

Weeks 4–10

Validate Your Market

Define your ICP, run customer discovery, test your assumptions. Come back with evidence, not opinions.

Every other week

Live Sessions

Walk through a module with Tim and the cohort. Workshop your answers, get unstuck in real time.

Every other week

Office Hours

Deep-dive coaching for 2-3 founders per session. Bring your hardest problem, leave with a next step.

Every week

Founder Workouts

A structured co-working hour. Show up, commit to one thing, get it done. Simple accountability that actually works.

3 Sessions

Expert AMAs

Curated conversations with operators and investors focused on where you are right now — sourcing customers, validating demand, positioning your offer.

Week 10

Pitch Competition

Put it all together. Present your pitch deck to a panel of mentors, investors, and peers. Walk away with real feedback — and the proof that you can tell your story.

This isn’t for everyone.

Everyone gets real attention, real feedback, and real accountability. That’s why we’re selective about who we bring in.

You’re a fit if:

You have a startup idea you’re serious about pursuing
You’re early-stage — pre-revenue, pre-product, or just getting started
You can commit ~5 hours/week for 10 weeks
You want to be pushed, not just supported

This probably isn’t for you if:

You’re looking for a course you can do passively
You already have a funded product in market
You’re not ready to put your idea in front of other people
